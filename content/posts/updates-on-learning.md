## Reading

I think this part here will be the hardest to conceptualize or to write down.
There are so many things that I want to read and there are so many things that I want to experience.
I find time and writing hard to digest in certain waves.
When I say waves, I am not meaning any actual motion, I mean more brain or perceived waves.
I struggle to find motivation even when reading something that I am enthralled in.
I find my brain will tell me that my time is being wasted or that I am not being particularly productive.
Reading is not the sole time this happens, its also quite prevenlent in any activity outside of work or being with my family.
I want to take the time to read more and to be more well read.
I want to not find the time to read, but read when I have extra time.
Time is not something I should *need* to find, its all around; I just need to allow myself the ability to use it as I truly want.

## Technology
I am currently working on this site.
I have learned something truly profound, I know nothing when it comes to web development! 
Currently I am also running through the idea of taking down my current home server and rebuilding inside of an actual rack mount enclosure so I have more bookshelf spaces for charging devices and books that look pretty(maybe some that I will actually read). 
Regarding my biggest change to learning is I am writing this via neovim.
I chose this as a way to show myself that I would not need any help from an IDE.
I want to be clear, I am not better than an IDE, I find it too focused on autocomplete and syntax-maxxing that I dont want to develop a crutch to it.
It's pretty easy once you get started and the hardest part, exiting, is just something that you have to embrace.
I have not left nvim in 4 months, I just keep opening new ones! (gotta use that RAM somehow)

## Life
This one is tough, learning in life is a constant flow that is driven by external moments.
I am always learning, whether or not that is good learning or bad, it is still learning.
Recently I have been haunted by thoughts that are neither constructive nor helpful.
I think the source of all of these are known, its just how I want to go at them.
Something that is not always obvious in life are the lasts.
Your last time at place before it closes down, the last time you get to hug your friend, the last message you send on your phone before it falls and you need to replace it.
I've always told myself I was aware of these and would never let it get me down, and my biggest lesson has been, you are never ready; and thats okay.

2026 started as "the year of intention" and I plan to maintain that; maybe through this here blog, maybe in my journal.
Intention is something that we all need in today's world; be intentional and present.
